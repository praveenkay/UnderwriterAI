ZURICH INSURANCE SME UNDERWRITING GUIDELINES

1. RENEWAL DISCOUNT POLICIES

For clients with clean claims history (no claims in past 2 years):
- Standard renewal discount: 10%
- Loyalty discount (3+ years): Additional 5%
- Risk profile improvement: Additional 2-5%

Restaurant businesses are eligible for:
- Equipment upgrade discount: 7% if kitchen equipment modernized
- Safety certification discount: 8% for fire safety compliance
- Staff training discount: 5% for food safety training completion

2. RISK ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

High-risk indicators requiring escalation:
- Previous claims exceeding £50,000
- Business operating in flood-prone areas
- Manufacturing with hazardous materials
- Hospitality venues with alcohol licensing issues

Medium-risk adjustments:
- Retail businesses: Standard rates with security requirements
- Professional services: Reduced rates for established practices
- Technology companies: Cyber security requirements mandatory

3. COVERAGE AMENDMENT RULES

Property coverage increases:
- Up to 20% increase: Automatic approval
- 20-50% increase: Manager approval required
- Above 50%: Underwriter review mandatory

Liability coverage adjustments:
- Professional indemnity: Minimum £1M for consultancy
- Public liability: £2M minimum for retail operations
- Product liability: Industry-specific requirements apply

4. ESCALATION PROCEDURES

Immediate escalation required for:
- Claims history showing pattern of losses
- Business expansion into new territories
- Change of business use or industry classification
- Premium increases exceeding 25%

Standard escalation process:
1. Senior underwriter review
2. Risk committee assessment if above £100K exposure
3. Regional manager approval for policy exceptions
4. Head office approval for high-value/complex risks

5. AUTOMATED DECISION THRESHOLDS

Auto-approve renewals when:
- No claims in past 3 years
- Premium under £5,000
- No significant business changes
- Risk profile stable or improved

Require manual review when:
- New claims reported since last renewal
- Business premises changed
- Ownership or management changes
- Industry risk profile changes

6. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

Seasonal businesses:
- Adjusted premium payment schedules available
- Coverage can be suspended during off-season
- Minimum annual premium applies

Multi-location businesses:
- Aggregated risk assessment required
- Location-specific adjustments apply
- Catastrophe risk modeling necessary

Technology sector:
- Cyber insurance mandatory for data processors
- Professional indemnity scaled to revenue
- Business interruption coverage critical

Manufacturing sector:
- Environmental liability assessment required
- Product recall coverage recommended
- Supply chain risk evaluation mandatory

This document serves as the primary reference for underwriting decisions in the SME division. All underwriters must familiarize themselves with these guidelines and escalate any decisions outside standard parameters.
